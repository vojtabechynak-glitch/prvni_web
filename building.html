Building Tips

🌲 Mix Wood Types for Depth

Using a single wood type makes builds look flat and repetitive. Mix Oak, Spruce, Dark Oak, and Acacia to add visual contrast. Use stairs and slabs instead of full blocks for roofs and overhangs — they create depth and prevent the "box" look that beginners often struggle with.

🪨 Combine Materials for Aesthetics

Great builds use at least 3–4 complementary materials. A classic combo is Spruce logs + Stone Bricks + Cobblestone + Oak Planks for a medieval style. Add glass pane windows to break up large walls, and use trapdoors, item frames, and banners as decorative details that add personality without bulk.

📐 Plan on Flat Ground First

Before starting a large project, find or create a flat area and lay out the footprint of your build using wool or concrete. This lets you visualise proportions before committing resources. Use odd numbers for width and height (5, 7, 9 blocks wide) — structures with odd dimensions tend to look more natural and balanced than even ones.

💡 Add Interior Lighting Creatively

Avoid placing bare torches everywhere — they break immersion. Instead use sea lanterns, lanterns, shroomlights, glowstone, or hide light sources inside blocks using end rod lighting or carpets over glowstone. For medieval builds, use iron bars as torch holders on walls for a classic dungeon look.

🏘️ Vary the Roofline

One of the most common beginner mistakes is building a flat or uniformly pitched roof. Add towers, dormers, and overhangs at different heights. A roofline with varying levels makes even a simple house look complex and interesting. Try combining Spruce stairs + mossy cobblestone walls for a classic fantasy-style roof trim.

💡 Pro Tip — Building Material Palettes

🌲 Medieval/Fantasy: Spruce logs, Stone Bricks, Mossy Cobblestone, Dark Oak planks
🏖️ Beach/Tropical: Sandstone, Acacia planks, Jungle trapdoors, Sea Lanterns
🏙️ Modern: Quartz blocks, White Concrete, Tinted Glass, Smooth Stone
🌿 Cozy Cottage: Oak planks, Mud Bricks, Birch slabs, Flower Pots + plants
